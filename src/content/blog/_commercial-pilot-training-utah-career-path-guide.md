Becoming a **commercial pilot** is a major step for anyone pursuing a professional aviation career. At Blitz Aviation, we provide structured programs that take you from private pilot certification to advanced commercial qualifications, all in **Northern Utah**.

## What Is a Commercial Pilot License (CPL)?

A **CPL** allows you to operate aircraft for compensation or hire. It is the key credential for those looking to fly professionally, whether for airlines, charter operations, or aerial services.

### FAA Requirements for a CPL

- Minimum of **18 years old**
- Hold a **Private Pilot License (PPL)**
- Possess at least a **second-class FAA medical certificate**
- Log a minimum of **250 flight hours**
- Complete approved flight training for commercial maneuvers
- Pass FAA written knowledge and practical tests

## Step-by-Step: Becoming a Commercial Pilot in Utah

### Step 1: Earn Your Private Pilot License

Before a CPL, you need a strong foundation with your **PPL**. Blitz Aviation helps you complete this efficiently.

### Step 2: Accumulate Flight Hours

Commercial training requires a minimum of **250 hours** of flight time, including cross-country, night, and instrument experience.

### Step 3: Instrument Rating (IFR Training)

An **instrument rating** enhances safety and makes you more marketable. Blitz Aviation’s IFR training prepares students for all-weather flight operations.

### Step 4: Enroll in Commercial Flight Training

Focus on advanced maneuvers, high-performance aircraft, and multi-engine operations if desired. Our instructors guide students step-by-step.

### Step 5: FAA Written Exam and Checkride

Passing the FAA written exam and **practical test** is your final step toward obtaining a CPL.

## Career Opportunities After CPL

- **Airline Transport Pilot (ATP) Pathway**
- **Charter and Corporate Flight Operations**
- **Flight Instruction (CFI/CFII)**
- **Aerial Surveying, Photography, or Agricultural Operations**
