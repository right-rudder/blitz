An **Instrument Rating (IFR)** is a crucial credential that allows pilots to operate safely in clouds, low visibility, and challenging weather conditions. At Blitz Aviation, our IFR training equips students in **Northern Utah** with the skills and confidence to fly beyond visual flight rules.

## Why Get an Instrument Rating?

- **Enhanced Safety**: Learn to navigate and control the aircraft when visibility is limited.
- **Career Advancement**: IFR-qualified pilots are more competitive for professional aviation jobs.
- **Expanded Flying Opportunities**: Avoid weather delays and fly more consistently year-round.

## FAA Requirements for an Instrument Rating

- Hold a **Private Pilot License (PPL)**
- Log at least **50 hours of cross-country flight as pilot in command**
- Accumulate **40 hours of actual or simulated instrument flight time**
- Pass the FAA **instrument rating written exam** and **practical test**

## Step-by-Step IFR Training in Utah

### Step 1: Ground School

Covers navigation, regulations, weather, instrument procedures, and flight planning.

### Step 2: Simulator and Flight Training

- Practice instrument approaches, holds, and cross-country flights
- Train in **Cessna 172 or Piper Cherokee** equipped with Garmin avionics
- Emphasis on real-world scenarios and safety

### Step 3: Night and Cross-Country Experience

- Build proficiency in diverse weather and terrain
- Learn to plan flights with alternate airports and fuel considerations

### Step 4: FAA Written Exam and Checkride

- Demonstrate knowledge of IFR procedures
- Show practical ability to handle simulated and real instrument conditions
